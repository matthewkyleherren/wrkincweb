People needed to send money home. No banks would reach them. So they made what they had work: airtime transfers via SMS. Safaricom saw the pattern, put a system to work around it, and launched M-Pesa in 2007. Then something unexpected happened. People started using airtime to buy things. A tomato. Onions for dinner. Airtime became currency. What began as a way to send money home exploded into everyday commerce. Today, nearly half of Kenya's GDPover $300 billion annuallydoes its work through it. 34 million users. 30 billion transactions a year. One of the largest mobile banking systems in the world, built on technology that already existed.
